In Summary

The Affordable Care Act is a piece of health care legislation that aims to overhaul the health system by increasing the access to health coverage.

It accomplishes this by:

  • Requiring U.S. citizens and legal citizens to have qualifying health coverage (with a few exceptions) or pay a tax penalty.
  • Expanding Medicaid to those below 133% of the poverty level
  • The creation of state-based exchanges where coverage policies can be purchased.
  • Addition of requirements and tax incentives for employers to offer health insurance to full-time employees.
  • Prohibiting denial of coverage based on pre-existing conditions
  • Allowing depdendents up to age 26 to stay on their parent's health insurance

It was signed into law in March of 2010 by President Barack Obama.

However, most of the major provisions specified above were not implemented until January 1st, 2014.

So the ACA technically happened in 2010, but it would be correft to state that it truly came into effect in 2014.
